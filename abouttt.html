Classification Scope

Our deep learning model is trained to recognize the four primary categories found in neurological MRI diagnostics.

Glioma

Tumors that start in the glial cells (support cells) of the brain or spine. They are the most common type of primary brain tumor and can vary in aggressiveness.

Meningioma

A tumor that arises from the meninges — the three layers of membrane that surround and protect the brain and spinal cord.

Pituitary Tumor

Abnormal growths that develop in your pituitary gland. While most are noncancerous, they can affect hormone levels and put pressure on the brain.

Healthy (No Tumor)

The absence of any identifiable tumorous growths. Identifying healthy scans is crucial for reducing false positives in diagnostic workflows.
